Resources for your four-legged friend

We understand that a new family member can be something of a handful. But that's where we'd like to help! Be sure to check out our fantastic tips and tricks for welcoming a new dog into your household environment. And if you'd like to do a bit of extra reading, stop by some of our recommended sites. Our tried-and-tested advice is guaranteed to put a smile on your face!



Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs):

Upon first bringing your dog home, it's best to arrange a physical exam with a local vet as soon as possible. This will give you a chance to register your pup with a professional provider, review your dog's health history, and discuss any concerns you may have. It's important that you continue to bring your furry friend in for a check up each year to ensure that there are no underlying issues. Your pup won't be able to tell you if something is wrong, so your best bet is to be proactive!

It can be difficult to get a footing on pet food when there are just so many options available! If you're bringing home a puppy, it's best to seek out commcercial "growth" food from a reputable brand. Certain dog breeds might require different kinds of nutrition depending on their size and dietary needs. There are a number of wonderful sites that provide valuable insight on this topic (see our section on additional resources). When in doubt, consult your veterinarian; they are happy to help!

Here are TinDog, we think it of the utmost importance that our furry friends are well-socialized. These positive experiences with humans and other dogs alike are critical in a dog's healthy development. From walking around the neighborhood to visiting local dog parks to enrolling in puppy classes, new environments with new people is the way to go! But of course, if your dog is visiting new places, it's best that they have some level of training! Walking on a leash, responding to cues of "sit", "stay", etc. are basic skills that we'd strongly recommend you work with. But remember: positive reinforcement (treats, praise) is truly the tried-and-tested method. So whether you're beginning your training at home or with a professional, make sure you are encouraging the behavior that you want to see!

Dogs are incredibly curious and sociable animals. They get attached to their humans, and they feel sad when their humans are gone! Thus if a pet dog is your calling, it's critical that you ensure that your lifestyle is ready to accommodate a fairly-needy family member. Much like small human children, our furry friends require both physical and mental exercise; whether it's going for a daily walk, playing a few games of chase or tug-of-war, or snuggling up on the couch, your pup will appreciate the time you two spend together. During times when you need to leave the house, be sure to provide safe chew toys, a food puzzle, or a visit from a dog walker if you'll be gone for the majority of the day. If you need to leave home for a period of travel, make plans for a qualified pet sitter to visit the house and provide care.

While the cost of caring for a pet can vary greatly from owner to owner, most aspiring dog owners should plan to spend upwards of $500-2,000 on the initial expenses to adopt, purchase supplies, and connect with a veterinarian for a new-puppy check up. Additional medical costs such as spaying/neutering, heartworm medication, etc. will further contribute to the bill, and if you seek pet insurance (which is generally recommended, as it resembles human health insurance), then that will incur additional costs. It then becomes necessary to account for money spent each month on replenishing food, toys, etc. And of course, if travel is in your future, you should also anticipate the cost of a pet sitter. Because of this, most pet owners find that a dog requires anywhere between $800-3,000 of annual care.
